Capital

Phnom Penh

Phnom Penh, Cambodia's busy capital, sits at the junction of the Mekong and Tonle Sap rivers. It was a hub for both the Khmer Empire and French colonialists. On its walkable riverfront, Lined with parks, restaurants and bars, are the ornate Royal Palace, Silver Pagoda and the national Museum, displaying artifacts from around the country. At the city's heart is the massive, art deco Central Market.

Ratanakiri is a province of northeast Cambodia. It borders the provinces of Mondulkiri to the south and Stung Treng to the west and the and the countries of Laos and Vietnam to the north and east,respectively. The province extends from the mountains of the annamite Range in the north,across a hilly plateau between the Tonle San and Tonle Srepok rivers.
